# PWA Skills Trial — Appendix E: Launch Mission Pack

This document outlines the standard content, scenario checks, and mock briefs for the 9 steps of the V2 Skills Trial.

---

## Step 1: Welcome to Pure Water
* **Kind:** `learn` | **Est. Time:** 30 min | **Day:** 1 | **Client:** PWA Team
* **Story:** *"Before you touch client work, meet the people it serves: pastors and ministry leaders drowning in admin. Every task you take has a congregation behind it."*
* **Core Learn Body:** Pure Water Automations supports pastors and ministry leaders by removing administrative burden through documented workflows and light automation. We expect VAs to execute reliably today, and search for workflow improvements tomorrow (Systems Scout).
* **Scenario Triage Question:** *"A client's weekly newsletter takes 3 hours because submissions arrive in 4 different formats. You're asked to produce this week's issue. What's the PWA move?"*
  * **Option A (Correct):** Produce it well, then note the intake problem and suggest a single submission form.
  * **Option B:** Build an automation to parse all 4 formats before doing the newsletter.
  * **Option C:** Just produce it — process improvement isn't the VA's job.
* **AI Feedback (Correct):** *"Exactly — execute reliably first, then scout the system. Simplify before automating."*
* **AI Feedback (Incorrect):** *"Not quite. Reliable delivery comes first, and simplifying beats automating — but noticing the pattern IS part of the job."*

## Step 2: Sandbox Console Practice
* **Kind:** `tour` | **Est. Time:** 45 min | **Day:** 1 | **Client:** PWA Team
* **Story:** *"The VA Manager is where the whole team lives. The habits you build in this sandbox are the habits clients feel as reliability."*
* **Instructions:** Candidates must practice executing a task lifecycle:
  1. Set status to "In Progress".
  2. Post a comment: *"Starting now — will flag anything unclear @teamlead"*.
  3. Attach an evidence URL (any link works in the sandbox).
  4. Click "Submit for review".

## Step 3: How We Keep Promises
* **Kind:** `learn` | **Est. Time:** 45 min | **Day:** 1 | **Client:** PWA Team
* **Story:** *"A client once nearly walked away — not over a mistake, but over silence. Learn the communication pattern that keeps trust."*
* **Scenario Triage Question:** *"It's 2 hours before a deliverable is due and you realize you'll miss it by half a day. What do you do?"*
  * **Option A:** Say nothing and push to finish as fast as possible — it's close.
  * **Option B (Correct):** Notify now with a revised ETA, what's done so far, and what you'll do differently.
  * **Option C:** Submit what you have without comment so something is on time.
* **AI Feedback (Correct):** *"Right. Early notice with a plan protects the relationship — that's the whole game."*
* **AI Feedback (Incorrect):** *"The team can absorb almost any delay it knows about in advance. Silence is the only unrecoverable option."*

## Step 4: AI and Safety Guidelines
* **Kind:** `learn` | **Est. Time:** 45 min | **Day:** 1 | **Client:** PWA Team
* **Story:** *"We use AI openly here. Learn what never goes into an AI tool — and what always gets verified before a client sees it."*
* **Scenario Triage Question:** *"A mock client's email accidentally includes a spreadsheet of donor names and amounts. Your task only needs the event date from that email. What do you do?"*
  * **Option A:** Use the date, and paste the email into AI to summarize the rest — it saves time.
  * **Option B (Correct):** Use the date, don't forward or paste the attachment anywhere, and flag the exposure to the team.
  * **Option C:** Delete the email and ask the client to resend everything.
* **AI Feedback (Correct):** *"Correct — minimum necessary use, no propagation, and a human flag. That's security by design."*
* **AI Feedback (Incorrect):** *"Careful — sensitive data never goes into AI tools or gets resent. Use only what the task needs and alert a person."*

## Step 5: Client Sim — Community Impact Day
* **Kind:** `sim` | **Est. Time:** 105 min | **Day:** 2 | **Client:** Grace Community Center
* **Story:** *"Tomorrow's newsletter reaches hundreds of families. The request is messy — a conflicting date, a missing link, an urgent ask. Exactly like real life."*
* **Mock Client Brief:** *"Please announce our Community Impact Day. It is Saturday, August 12 from 10:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. at the community center. The flyer says August 21, but I think the flyer might be old. We need this in the newsletter and calendar today. Registration is required. Please make it sound exciting and send it out as soon as possible."*
* **Expected High-Quality Behaviors:**
  * Candidate identifies the flyer date conflict (August 21 vs August 12).
  * Candidate asks client to confirm the date and request the missing registration link before drafting a final version.
  * Candidate flags unconfirmed dates in their draft as placeholders (e.g. `[DATE TBC]`).
  * Candidate transitions status to "Blocked — awaiting client" and submits a handoff note.

## Step 6: Specialization Challenge
* **Kind:** `branch` | **Est. Time:** 120 min | **Day:** 3 | **Client:** Assigned track
* **Briefs:**
