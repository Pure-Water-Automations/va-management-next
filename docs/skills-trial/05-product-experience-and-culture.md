# PWA Skills Trial — Phase 5: Product Experience & Culture

## 1. Product Experience Pillars

### Work feels meaningful
Missions always lead with a business story. We explain **why** the task is being done (e.g. helping a pastor connect with their congregation) rather than treating it as an academic assignment.

### Trust is earned through responsibility
Instead of virtual coins, XP, or badges, progress is shown as climbing the "Responsibility Ladder." The reward for good work is access to more trust and higher-tier client responsibilities.

### Coaching over criticism
Feedback uses positive developmental framing:
1. **Observation:** *"I noticed the date on the flyer doesn't match the email notes."*
2. **Impact:** *"If published, this could confuse parishioners."*
3. **Suggestion:** *"Confirm the date with the client before finalizing the draft."*
4. **Encouragement:** *"Your written announcement style is excellent; this correction will make it ready."*

## 2. Calm UX Design Principles
The candidate interface should feel clean, professional, and minimal (inspired by Linear, Apple, and Arc).
* **Typography:** Modern sans-serif (Inter/Outfit), clean layout.
* **Palette:** Pure Water's navy blue, sky blue accents, warm white backgrounds.
* **Animations:** Subtle transitions (no flashy elements, coins, or pressure-inducing streaks).
* **AI Disclosure:** Clearly state Purii is an AI assistant, and provide a single click path to human review.
