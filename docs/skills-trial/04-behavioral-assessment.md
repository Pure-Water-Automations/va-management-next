# PWA Skills Trial — Phase 4: Behavioral Assessment Engine

## 1. Competency Taxonomy
The system collects objective evidence across 6 core operational competencies.

### 1. Reliability & Commitments
* **Definition:** Honoring commitments, showing up prepared, and communicating early when delays occur.
* **Positive Signals:** Attending meetings, submitting on time, responding during declared work windows, scheduling standups early.
* **Red Flags:** Missed standup without notice, missing check-in deadlines, disappearing during active work sessions.

### 2. Communication & Escalation
* **Definition:** Keeping team members informed, reducing operational uncertainty, and expressing boundaries politely.
* **Positive Signals:** Asking clarifying questions about date conflicts, reporting blockers before deadlines, using professional client-safe tone.
* **Red Flags:** Making assumptions about unclear briefs, defending mistakes, or ignoring requests for updates.

### 3. Ownership & Recovery
* **Definition:** Taking charge of successful project outcomes and correcting course responsibly when errors occur.
* **Positive Signals:** Creating a draft revision plan, implementing all feedback items, providing a handoff update.
* **Red Flags:** Submitting incomplete work, neglecting review requests, or waiting passively when blocked.

### 4. Coachability & Feedback Response
* **Definition:** Receptive response to correction and constructive critique.
* **Positive Signals:** Stating exact ETA revisions, implementing all feedback items, asking for verification.
* **Red Flags:** Repeating identical mistakes on resubmission, ignoring requested changes, or arguing defensively.

### 5. Systems Thinking (Systems Scout)
* **Definition:** Mapping steps and suggesting optimizations for repetitive processes.
* **Positive Signals:** Writing structured SOPs (Purpose, Inputs, Steps, Exceptions, DoD), identifying bottlenecks, proposing sensible automations.
* **Red Flags:** Working purely task-by-task without recognizing process patterns.

### 6. Professionalism & Integrity
* **Definition:** Operating with honesty, confidentiality, and respect.
* **Positive Signals:** Disclosing limited specialization experience (positive candor), protecting synthetic mock data.
* **Critical Flags:** Falsifying links (e.g. pasting empty Google docs), plagiarizing answers, or using inappropriate tone.

---

## 2. Confidence Indicators
To prevent AI overreach, the evidence summary qualifies its findings:
* **Low Confidence:** Only 1 or 2 isolated observations.
* **Medium Confidence:** Multiple observations across different days.
* **High Confidence:** A repeated pattern observed in both core and branch challenges, backed by consistent timing and messages.
