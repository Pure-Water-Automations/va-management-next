# PWA Skills Trial — Phase 1: Product Vision & Philosophy

## 1. Executive Summary
The **PWA Skills Trial** replaces the traditional "10-Hour Training" with a carefully designed **work simulation** that immerses candidates in a realistic first week at Pure Water Automations.

Instead of asking, *"Can this person complete a few assignments?"*, the Skills Trial answers:
> **"Would we trust this person to work with a real client?"**

Every design decision flows from that question. The trial identifies remote operators who demonstrate:
* Reliability
* Ownership
* Communication
* Curiosity
* Systems thinking
* Coachability
* Professionalism

---

## 2. Product Philosophy

### Teach through work
Knowledge is introduced while solving meaningful, contextualized problems. We do not use passive tutorials; learning is active and operational.

### Evaluate through behavior
Behavior predicts long-term success better than skill attestation. We observe responsiveness, accuracy, attention to detail, and error recovery.

### Coach through AI
AI reduces repetitive manual coordination and mentoring, providing first-pass checks and encouraging iterative refinement.

### Decide through humans
Hiring decisions remain human responsibilities. The system compiles evidence; humans review and decide.

---

## 3. The North Star
> **Every feature should make the candidate feel more like a new employee and less like a student.**

If an interaction feels like school (e.g., standard multiple-choice quizzes, watching generic lectures), we reconsider it. If it feels like meaningful work (resolving a conflicting date, preparing a newsletter draft), it is aligned with PWA.

---

## 4. Primary Success Metrics
* **90-Day Retention:** Percentage of hired VAs active and successful after 90 days.
* **Manager Satisfaction:** Rating of VA operational capability in their first month.
* **Reviewer Time Saved:** Reduction in hours Eunmi/HR spend chasing updates and compiling rubrics.
* **Missed Commitments:** Reduction in unnotified absent standups or delayed tasks.
